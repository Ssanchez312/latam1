Welcome to Ecuador

Ecuador is a country of rich history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant cultural traditions. Nestled in South America, it borders Colombia and Peru. Known as the "Middle of the World," its diverse geography includes the Amazon rainforest, the Andes mountains, picturesque colonial cities, and the breathtaking Galápagos Islands, making it one of the most fascinating destinations in Latin America.


Why Visit?

Ecuador offers an authentic and diverse travel experience, whether you’re looking to explore ancient ruins, hike majestic volcanoes, relax on stunning beaches, or immerse yourself in indigenous traditions. Its friendly people, rich history, and breathtaking landscapes make it a must-visit destination in Latin America.

Must-Visit Destinations

Half of the world

Mitad del Mundo is one of Ecuador’s most iconic landmarks, marking the equatorial line that divides the Northern and Southern Hemispheres. Visitors can stand with one foot in each hemisphere, explore the monument, and visit interactive museums that showcase Ecuador’s rich cultural and scientific history. The surrounding area offers local artisan shops, traditional cuisine, and exhibits that highlight the country’s indigenous heritage and geographical significance.

Galapagos Islands

The Galápagos Islands are a world-renowned destination known for their unique wildlife, pristine beaches, and breathtaking landscapes. This volcanic archipelago offers visitors a chance to explore its diverse ecosystems, home to giant tortoises, marine iguanas, and blue-footed boobies. The islands provide opportunities for snorkeling, hiking, and guided wildlife tours. Visitors can also experience the local culture, enjoy fresh seafood, and learn about the islands’ rich history and conservation efforts.

Yasuní National Park

Yasuní National Park is a biodiversity hotspot located in the heart of the Ecuadorian Amazon. Home to an incredible variety of wildlife, including jaguars, pink river dolphins, and hundreds of bird species, it offers visitors a truly immersive rainforest experience. Travelers can explore the park by canoe, hike through lush jungle trails, and visit indigenous communities that have preserved their ancestral traditions. Yasuní’s unparalleled ecological richness and cultural significance make it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Cajas National Park

A breathtaking natural reserve characterized by stunning mountain landscapes, glacial lakes, and diverse ecosystems, Cajas National Park is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Located in the Ecuadorian Andes, the park features over 200 lakes, cloud forests, and high-altitude grasslands. Visitors can enjoy hiking through scenic trails, spotting unique wildlife such as llamas and Andean condors, and experiencing the tranquility of this pristine environment. The park’s dramatic scenery and rich biodiversity make it a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
